# Workload Distribution

## Global Compute Workload Distribution

<iframe src="./main.html" height="550" width="100%" scrolling="no"></iframe>

[View Workload Distribution MicroSim Fullscreen](./main.html){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

This interactive chart shows how global computational workloads are distributed
across major categories and highlights the tiny fraction that quantum computing
could potentially address. Use the toggle buttons to switch between a doughnut
chart of overall workload distribution and a stacked horizontal bar chart that
breaks out the "potentially QC-addressable" slice for each category. Click on
any segment or bar to see a detailed explanation of why quantum computing does
or does not apply to that workload type.

Even under optimistic assumptions, quantum computing is relevant to less than
2% of all computation performed worldwide. The overwhelming majority of global
compute -- web serving, AI training, databases, and business applications --
runs on workloads where quantum computers offer no theoretical advantage
whatsoever.
