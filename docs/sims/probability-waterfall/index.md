# Probability Waterfall

This MicroSim uses a waterfall-style bar chart to show how joint probability
collapses when multiple independent breakthroughs are each required for
quantum computing to become economically viable.

Each bar represents the cumulative probability remaining after one more
breakthrough requirement is factored in. Because the probabilities multiply,
even generous individual estimates (20-50% each) produce a vanishingly
small joint probability when ten breakthroughs are all required.

Use the dropdown to switch between four scenarios ranging from pessimistic
to proponent-level optimism, and observe that the final probability remains
extremely low in every case.
