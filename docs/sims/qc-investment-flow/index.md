# QC Investment Flow

This Sankey diagram traces the flow of capital through the quantum computing
ecosystem, from funding sources on the left, through research and operational
categories in the middle, to measurable outputs on the right.

The key visual takeaway is stark: approximately $105 billion in cumulative
investment flows in from governments, corporations, venture capital, SPACs,
and other sources. That capital spreads across hardware research, algorithm
development, software tools, cloud infrastructure, operations, and salaries.
Yet the revenue output on the far right is a barely visible sliver — roughly
$50 million, or about 0.05% of total investment.

## How to Read This Diagram

- **Left column (Sources):** Where the money originates. Government grants
  are the largest source, followed by corporate R&D budgets.
- **Middle column (Destinations):** Where the money is spent. Hardware
  research dominates, consuming nearly half of all funding.
- **Right column (Outputs):** What the spending produces. Papers and patents
  are abundant; revenue is negligible.

The color of each flow reflects its funding source, while the output
flows use green for research outputs and red for the thin revenue stream.
