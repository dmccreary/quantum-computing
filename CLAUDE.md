# Quantum Computing Textbook - Claude Guidelines

## Learning Mascot: Fermi the Ferret

### Character Overview

- **Name**: Fermi
- **Species**: Ferret
- **Personality**: Curious but relentless, sharp-witted, demands evidence, dry humor
- **Catchphrase**: "But does the math check out?"
- **Visual**: Sleek brown-and-cream ferret with small round glasses and an indigo vest with orange pocket square. No held objects.

### Voice Characteristics

- Uses direct, analytical language with occasional dry wit
- Occasionally uses investigation/detective metaphors ("Let's sniff this out")
- Refers to students as "fellow investigators"
- Signature phrases: "Show me the numbers.", "Let's sniff this out.", "Interesting claim — where's the evidence?", "That doesn't add up.", "But does the math check out?"

### Placement Rules

| Context | Admonition Type | Frequency |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| General note / sidebar | mascot-neutral | As needed |
| Chapter opening | mascot-welcome | Every chapter |
| Key concept | mascot-thinking | 2-3 per chapter |
| Helpful tip | mascot-tip | As needed |
| Common mistake | mascot-warning | As needed |
| Section completion | mascot-celebration | End of major sections |
| Difficult content | mascot-encourage | Where students may struggle |

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Use Fermi to introduce new topics with healthy skepticism
- Include the catchphrase in welcome admonitions
- Keep dialogue brief (1-3 sentences)
- Match the pose/image to the content type

**Don't:**

- Use Fermi more than 5-6 times per chapter
- Put mascot admonitions back-to-back
- Use the mascot for purely decorative purposes
- Change Fermi's personality or speech patterns
- Have Fermi hold objects in image prompts (images are too small)
